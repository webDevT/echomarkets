What are
Swap Rates?

A swap rate, also referred to as a rollover rate or simply a swap, is the overnight interest added or deducted for holding a position open overnight. Swaps are a fee that is applied to open trades at the end of a trading session. With a swap rate, traders have the ability to extend their trades into the next session without the need to close or settle them.

How are
Swap Rates calculated?

A swap rate is calculated based on the difference in interest rates between the two currencies in a currency pair. For example, if you are trading the Euro against the US Dollar (EUR/USD), the swap rate would consider the interest rates of both the European Union and the United States.

Likewise, the WTI swap rate is calculated based on the difference in interest rates between the currency in which the WTI contract is priced and the currency of your account. For example, if an Australian trader holds a long position in WTI priced in USD, the swap rate calculation will take into account the interest rate difference between USD and AUD. Swap rates are applied whether a position is long or short.
