What is forex
trading?

Forex trading, otherwise known as FX or currency trading, refers to the process of buying and selling global currencies. Always priced in currency pairs, the global forex market is the largest, most liquid, truly decentralised market on the planet. With an estimated daily turnover of over $5 trillion, it operates 24 hours a day, five days a week and thus affords you consistent trading opportunities. The most widely traded forex markets are called the ‘majors’, including popular currency pairs such as EUR/USD, GBP/USD and AUD/USD.

How does forex trading work?

By buying and selling one currency priced in relation to another on your Echo Markets account, you’re able to profit from price fluctuations in the market. The first currency in any pair is always known as the 'base currency', while the second is called the 'quote currency'. Forex currency pairs are displayed in terms of how many units of the quote currency you can buy with one unit of the base currency. Using the highly liquid EUR/USD as an example, 1.10000 means that 1 Euro is priced at 1.10 US Dollars.

Forex trading examples

Selling EUR/USD

The gross profit on your trade is calculated as follows:

Opening Price

€200,000 x 1.33623 = USD $267,246

Closing Price

€200,000 x 1.32129 = USD $264,258

Gross Profit on Trade

$2988

Opening the position

The price of the Euro against the US Dollar (EUR/USD) is 1.33623/1.33624 and you decide to sell 2 standard lots (the equivalent of €200,000) at 1.33623.

The value of your position is €200,000 x 1.33623 = USD $267,246. The leverage on your trading acount is 1:30 therefore the margin required to poen the position is USD $267,246 / 30 = USD $8,908.20.

One week later the Euro has fallen against the US Dollar to 1.32128/1.32129 and you decide to take your profit by buying back 2 standard lots at 1.32129.
